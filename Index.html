What is TaeKwonDo

Taekwondo is a Korean martial art characterized by its emphasis on high, fast kicks and dynamic movements. Developed during the mid-20th century, it combines elements of traditional Korean martial arts, Chinese martial arts, and indigenous Korean fighting styles. Taekwondo practitioners, known as "taekwondoin," follow a strict code of ethics and principles, promoting values such as courtesy, integrity, perseverance, self-control, and indomitable spirit. The sport is not only a means of self-defense but also a path to personal development, physical fitness, and mental discipline. Taekwondo is practiced worldwide and is recognized for its distinctive kicking techniques, patterns (known as "poomsae"), and sparring (known as "kyorugi") competitions.
